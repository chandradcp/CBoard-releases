CalBoard Privacy Policy


_Last updated: 17 July 2026_
CalBoard is a keyboard (input method) for Android. This policy explains what the app does with your information. **This is a technical description of how the app behaves; the app owner is responsible for reviewing and publishing the final policy.**

The short version


  • What you type is used to draw the keyboard and to offer predictions **on your device**. It is not collected by CalBoard or sent to us.
  • CalBoard contains **no advertising and no analytics or tracking SDKs**. We do not build a profile of you.
  • Two features send information off your device, and only when you choose to use them: **voice typing** (handled by your device's own speech service) and **translation** (sent to a third‑party translation service). Both are described below.

    • What stays on your device


  • **Your typing.** Text prediction, autocorrect, and the personal dictionary run locally, using on‑device dictionaries and small on‑device language models. Words you type may be saved to a personal dictionary **on your device** to improve future suggestions. You can clear it in Settings → Dictionary.
  • **Contacts (optional).** If you grant the Contacts permission, CalBoard reads your contact **names** so it can suggest and correctly spell them. This happens on your device; contact data is not uploaded. If you deny or revoke the permission, the feature turns off.
  • **Settings.** Your preferences (theme, layout, toggles) are stored locally on your device.

    • Information that leaves your device


    CalBoard only sends data off your device for the two features below, and only when you actively use them.
    ### Voice typing (microphone)
    When you tap the microphone, CalBoard uses **Android's built‑in speech recognition service** to convert speech to text. The audio is processed by that system service (on many devices this is provided by Google), not by CalBoard, and CalBoard does not store your audio. Whether recognition happens on‑device or in the cloud is determined by your device's speech service and its own privacy policy. If you never use voice typing, the microphone is never used.
    ### Translation
    The translation feature has its own separate input box. Text you type **into that box** is sent over the internet to **MyMemory** (`api.mymemory.translated.net`), a third‑party translation service, to return a translation. This only happens for text you enter in the translation panel and only when you request a translation — your normal typing into other apps is never sent there. MyMemory processes that text under its own privacy policy ().

    Children


    CalBoard is a general‑purpose utility and is not directed at children.

    Data sharing and selling


    CalBoard does not sell your data and does not share it with third parties for advertising. The only third parties that receive any data are the device speech service (for voice typing) and the translation service (for translation), as described above, and only to provide those features.

    Your controls


  • Turn voice typing on or off in Settings → Voice Typing.
  • Turn the Contacts feature on or off by granting or revoking the Contacts permission.
  • Clear your personal dictionary in Settings → Dictionary.
  • Uninstalling CalBoard removes all of its locally stored data from your device.
